Tourism & Culture

What there is to see

Beyond the finish line, Mbololo / Ronge has its own reasons to linger.

Mbololo Forest

The highest peak of its massif at roughly 2,209m, and one of the richest birdwatching sites in the Taita Hills.

Ronge ridge

A quieter, less-visited forest-guard station area on the same conservation network as Mbololo and Ngangao.

Conservation

Why the terrain is protected

Every leg of the tour runs through land under active conservation — here's what that looks like locally.

Endemic bird habitat

Mbololo is critical habitat for the Taita Thrush and Taita Apalis — two bird species found nowhere else on Earth, both flagship species for the Eastern Arc Mountains biodiversity hotspot.

Forest guard network

Mbololo and Ronge are both staffed Kenya Forest Service stations, part of the coordinated patrol network protecting the Taita Hills' remaining forest fragments.
