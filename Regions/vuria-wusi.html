Leg 02 · Vuria / Wusi

Sat 12 – Mon 14 Dec 2026  ·  Date confirmed
The Route

Course & profile

This leg tops out near Vuria Peak, the county's highest point at 2,228m, reached via Wundanyi and Mwanda. Route maps for this leg are finalised and reconfirmed with the county and local conservancies before each edition — check back closer to race day for the exact GPX file and road closures.
Tourism & Culture

What there is to see

Beyond the finish line, Vuria / Wusi has its own reasons to linger.

Vuria Peak

The highest point in Taita Taveta County at 2,228m, with a bare rock-ledge summit offering a 360° viewing deck — Kenya Forest Service permits camping at the top.

Wusi / Kishamba villages

Terraced hillside farms and one of the distinct Dawida dialect communities, reached along the Wundanyi–Mwanda road.

Conservation

Why the terrain is protected

Every leg of the tour runs through land under active conservation — here's what that looks like locally.

Vuria montane forest remnant

A heavily logged but recovering ~51-hectare forest patch below the summit, managed by the Kenya Forest Service as part of ongoing restoration efforts.

Ridge water catchment

Vuria's slopes feed several of the springs that supply the lower Taita Hills — a working example of hilltop forest as water infrastructure.
