The Route

Course & profile

A gentler leg through Bura's terraced farmland, finishing at the historic mission grounds. Route maps for this leg are finalised and reconfirmed with the county and local conservancies before each edition — check back closer to race day for the exact GPX file and road closures.
Tourism & Culture

What there is to see

Beyond the finish line, Bura has its own reasons to linger.

Bura Mission Church

A historic colonial-era mission landmark and one of the area's oldest standing buildings, at the heart of the Bura community.

Terraced hillside farms

Bura's slopes are intensively terraced for maize, banana and vegetable farming — a working agricultural landscape as much as a scenic one.

Conservation

Why the terrain is protected

Every leg of the tour runs through land under active conservation — here's what that looks like locally.

Hillside forest patches

Bura retains small gazetted forest fragments within the wider Taita Hills network, sustaining local water sources.

Community agroforestry

Long-standing terracing and agroforestry practices in Bura double as soil and water conservation measures on steep terrain.
