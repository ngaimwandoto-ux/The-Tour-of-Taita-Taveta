Tourism & Culture

What there is to see

Beyond the finish line, Mbale has its own reasons to linger.

Wesu Rock

A granite outcrop just outside Wundanyi with panoramic views over the Taita Hills and plains — best at sunrise or sunset.

Taita Skull Caves & Ngangao Forest

The traditional 'Cave of Skulls' burial site and Ngangao's cloud forest, the largest forest fragment in the main Taita massif, known for its butterflies.

Conservation

Why the terrain is protected

Every leg of the tour runs through land under active conservation — here's what that looks like locally.

Ngangao Forest

At roughly 92–149 hectares, the largest remaining forest fragment in the Taita Hills, sheltering endemic birds alongside Mbololo and Chawia.

Taita Research Station

A University of Helsinki field station based in Wundanyi studying land change, biodiversity and water resources across the Taita Hills.
