The Route

Course & profile

Kasighau's route skirts Rukinga Wildlife Sanctuary before climbing toward Mount Kasigau's lower springs. Route maps for this leg are finalised and reconfirmed with the county and local conservancies before each edition — check back closer to race day for the exact GPX file and road closures.
Tourism & Culture

What there is to see

Beyond the finish line, Kasighau has its own reasons to linger.

Mount Kasigau

An isolated massif south of the main Taita Hills, with footpaths climbing to springs and rock pools near the summit — home to the endemic Taita White-eye.

Wakasighau culture

Kasigau's community speaks its own distinct dialect, separate from the main Dawida-area dialects of the Taita Hills.

Conservation

Why the terrain is protected

Every leg of the tour runs through land under active conservation — here's what that looks like locally.

Rukinga Wildlife Sanctuary

Anchor of the Kasigau Corridor REDD+ project — eleven ranches across roughly 2,023 km² protecting dryland forest and offsetting an estimated one million tonnes of CO₂ a year.

Tsavo wildlife corridor

Rukinga forms a key dispersal corridor linking Tsavo East and Tsavo West National Parks for elephant and other wildlife movement.
